a testing machine

set up once. then every idea is 5 minutes from live.

5 minute offers takes an offer - a product, a design, an angle - from idea to live-and-buyable, with distribution posted, in about five minutes per drop. it does not find you a winning product. nothing does. it makes losing cheap enough that you can afford to be wrong thirty times - and thirty real attempts is how winners actually get found.

what this sells is speed of testing. shots on goal. nothing else is promised.

the problem it solves

nobody fails from a lack of tutorials. people fail because testing one offer takes them three weeks, so they test twice, miss twice, and quit. the person who can test twenty offers beats the person who perfects one. every time.

most people do not fail because they move too slow to win. they fail because they move too slow to lose enough times.

the modules

one module = one specific offer type, end to end. every field you would ever type by hand has a prompt. no cross-referencing, no guessing - open the module, follow it down, ship the drop.

the operating rules

  1. first, not best. decision rules replace taste. the first concept that clears the bar ships.
  2. the clock is the boss. polish is procrastination wearing a nice shirt.
  3. losers die on schedule. the 48-hour rule decides, not you.
  4. log everything. the log is the asset. the drops are just data.
  5. never promise outcomes. not to buyers, not to yourself. promise speed.
