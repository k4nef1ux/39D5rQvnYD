pricing rules - no thinking allowed

pricing is a rule, not a decision.

pathrulefloor
pod(base cost + shipping) x 3, rounded up to .99$8 margin - under it, change the product, never the margin
stickerspack pricing - see module 01$5 pack margin
dropshipcost x 2.5 - 3, shipping baked in, rounded to .99customer sees free shipping; the price already paid for it

the three laws

  1. never discount on drop day. a coupon is an iterate-verdict tool, not a launch tool. launching with a discount teaches the offer to beg
  2. never price under the floor to chase a sale. a sale with no margin is a loss wearing makeup
  3. round to .99, always. consistency reads as intentional; intentional reads as trustworthy

kill or scale - the 48-hour rule

this rule is the actual secret of the whole system. everyone sells starting. nobody sells quitting fast. speed only compounds if losers die on schedule - and the rule decides, not you. you will want to give a drop one more day. that want is the thing this section exists to kill.

verdictcondition at hour 48action
kill (default)under 10 link clicks, or 50+ clicks with zero sales and zero cartsarchive the product, log the verdict, next drop. no mourning
iterate10 - 50 clicks, carts or 1 salethe angle caught, the offer missed. run vault 4.1, relaunch as a NEW drop. max 2 iterations, then kill
scale2+ organic sales in 48hrelease remaining content, double next week's batch, consider $10 - 20/day behind the best short. it has earned one week

low-ticket offers (stickers) use the adjusted thresholds inside their module.

reading the clicks

  • no clicks: the hook failed, not the product. the content never earned the tap
  • clicks, no carts: the page failed - price shock, weak bullets, or a hook the page did not match
  • carts, no sales: friction or trust - shipping surprise, thin about page, price just over the line
  • sales: feed it. winners are fed, not admired

a drop that took 40 minutes and died in 48 hours is the system working. it cost one evening to learn what the three-week version pays a month for. log it, say so in public, pull the handle again.

the weekly ritual - 20 minutes, same day every week

  1. every open drop gets its 48h verdict rendered. no exceptions, no extensions
  2. run vault 4.2 (the autopsy) on the week's rows
  3. run vault 4.4 (the receipts post) and publish it
  4. write next week's 3 - 5 planned drops as empty rows. a row waiting is a commitment

what the log becomes: week 1, a habit. week 4, a pattern detector. week 8, your content engine. week 12, the receipts that sell whatever you build next.

the receipts loop - the log becomes the marketing

every drop generates a public artifact. the machine running in public is the most magnetic content in this space - and it costs nothing extra, because the log already wrote it. the discipline is the flex. never the money.

the drop card - every drop, day one

drop #14. nurses x dark humor, sticker 6-pack. built in 9:41. live. 48-hour clock started.

that is the whole post. the number and the stopwatch are the hook. people follow counters.

the verdict card - every drop, hour 48

drop #14, hour 48: 22 clicks, 2 carts, 0 sales. verdict: iterate - the angle caught, the shipping line killed the carts. relaunching as drop #15.

losing in public with a rule attached reads as competence, not failure. it is the exact post nobody else has the stomach to write - which is why it works.

the three laws of the loop

  1. no income claims. ever. the stopwatch and the verdicts are the whole show
  2. post the losses with the same energy as the wins. the credibility compounds off the kills
  3. never break the counter. drop numbers are sacred and sequential. an audience that watches #23 become #24 is an audience waiting for the next one

after 30 logged, posted drops you own three things nobody can copy: a pattern-tested niche map, an audience that watched you do the work, and the receipts to sell whatever you build next - without ever having promised anyone a dime.

friction fixes - getting to 5:00

the gap between drop 1 (forty minutes) and drop 15 (five) is just friction removal. run vault 4.5 on your timed steps weekly; the usual suspects:

the 48h check never happensno ritualcalendar block, same day weekly, 20 minutes. verdicts render or the system is dead
second-guessing the picktaste sneaking backreread the operating rules on the home page. first, not best

when a drop goes over 15 minutes after drop 5

stop optimizing the step and check the rule instead. overtime after drop 5 is almost never tooling - it is a decision that should have been a rule. find the decision you keep re-making, write the rule, add it to your copy of this page.

the graduation test

run a full drop, idea to completed, under 5:00, twice in one week. when that happens the machine is built - and the interesting question stops being "how fast" and becomes "which 5 cells next week." that question is answered by the log, which by now is smarter than both of us.
